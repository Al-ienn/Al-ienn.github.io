Sites that you may find useful

  • Wattpad

    Well-known site where you can publish and read stories of other authors, mostly not published. Also has an app for popular softwares. Using Wattpad is free, however, you need to sign up.

  • Archive of our own (AO3)

    Website where you can publish and read stories. Only available in web, with no need to sign up. Totally free. Most stories also have tags showing for which audience is this work, if it's finished or ongoing.

  • Campfire

    Site for writers to write their story, worldbuild and create characters. You need to register and choose a plan. It has free option, but it's limited to 25k words, 10 characters profiles and so on.

  • Fighters block

    It's a funny website where you set target word count and write, while character above figths a monster. Don't worry, you can always pause it and catch a break.

  • Webtoon

    Site with so called webtoons, where you read and publish comics legally. App is also available to download. You don't need to register to read. Audience is larger than on Tapas and you can find more topics there.

  • Tapas

    Antoher site with comics, but some novels can also be found. Comics are free but some restrictions may apply after few chapters. Tapas is also less restrictive than Webtoon when it comes to scenes for older audience. App is available.

  • Hemingwayapp

    Writing tool that suggests edits for text that you pasted. Free without need to register, but works only for texts written in english.

  • WordKeeperAlpha

    Progress tracker. Set your goal of words and organize your project. It also creates graphs of statistics for you. Free, but with need to register with e-mail.

  • r/writing

    A big subreddit for discusions about writing.
